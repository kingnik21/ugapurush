Subhas Chandra Bose

(1897-1945)


Leader of the Indian National Army (INA):
Bose is best known for his role as the leader of the Indian National Army Which also known as 'AZAD HIND SENA', which he formed in 1942 in Southeast Asia with the aim of overthrowing British rule in India. The INA played a significant role in the freedom struggle, capturing Andaman and Nicobar Islands from the British.

Founder of Forward Bloc:
Subhas Chandra Bose founded the Forward Bloc in 1939 after resigning from the Indian National Congress. The Forward Bloc was a political party that aimed to unite like-minded anti-British forces.

Civil Services Examination:
Bose cleared the Indian Civil Services Examination in 1920, but he resigned from the prestigious position in protest against British colonial policies and joined the freedom movement.

President of Indian National Congress:
Bose was elected as the President of the Indian National Congress in 1938 and 1939. His presidency marked a period of intense activism and anti-colonial fervor within the Congress party.

Formation of Azad Hind Government:
Bose established the Provisional Government of Free India (Azad Hind Government) in Singapore in 1943. He declared war against Britain and the United States, seeking help from Axis powers during World War II to free India from British rule.

Rallied International Support:
Bose traveled extensively, seeking international support for India's independence struggle. He met with leaders like Adolf Hitler and Benito Mussolini to garner backing for the cause of Indian independence.

Legacy:
Subhas Chandra Bose's legacy as a charismatic and passionate leader who believed in decisive action and total mobilization for independence continues to inspire generations of Indians. His contributions to the freedom struggle are remembered with respect and admiration.
