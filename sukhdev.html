Sukhdev Thapar

(1907-1931)


Member of Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA):
Sukhdev Thapar was a key member of the HSRA, a revolutionary organization in British India that aimed to overthrow British rule through armed struggle.

Lahore Conspiracy Case (1929):
Sukhdev was one of the accused in the Lahore Conspiracy Case, along with Bhagat Singh and Rajguru, for their involvement in the assembly bombing incident in the Central Legislative Assembly in Delhi.

Jallianwala Bagh Massacre (1919):
Sukhdev, like many other freedom fighters, was deeply affected by the Jallianwala Bagh massacre in Amritsar, which fueled his passion for the independence movement.

Role in the Assembly Bombing Incident (1929):


Sukhdev, along with Bhagat Singh and Rajguru, threw non-lethal bombs in the Central Legislative Assembly to protest against repressive laws, drawing attention to the need for independence.

Support for Bhagat Singh:
Sukhdev Thapar was a close associate of Bhagat Singh and supported him in his revolutionary activities. He was actively involved in planning and executing various actions against the British colonial administration.

Execution and Martyrdom (1931):
Sukhdev Thapar, along with Bhagat Singh and Rajguru, was sentenced to death for their involvement in the killing of British police officer James A. Saunders. They were executed on March 23, 1931, in Lahore Central Jail. Their martyrdom had a profound impact on the Indian independence movement, inspiring generations of Indians to continue the struggle for freedom.
